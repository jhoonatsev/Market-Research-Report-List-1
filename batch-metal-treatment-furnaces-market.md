Atmosphere furnaces utilize gases to create a protective atmosphere, preventing oxidation and contamination during heating. In contrast, vacuum furnaces operate under reduced pressure, ensuring high purity and precise temperature control, making them ideal for sensitive materials. Both furnace types cater to various applications, such as hardening, annealing, and sintering, and are critical in industries like aerospace and automotive.</p></p>
