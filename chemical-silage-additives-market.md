<p><p>The chemical silage additives market primarily comprises organic and inorganic acids, which enhance the fermentation process and improve the stability of silage. Organic acids, such as lactic acid and acetic acid, promote the growth of desirable microorganisms while inhibiting spoilage organisms, leading to better nutrient retention. Inorganic acids, including formic acid and propionic acid, primarily focus on reducing pH and controlling aerobic spoilage. Both types are crucial for optimizing silage quality, extending shelf life, and ensuring efficient feed utilization in livestock.</p></p>
