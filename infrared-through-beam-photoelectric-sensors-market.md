<p><p>Infrared through beam photoelectric sensors operate by emitting an infrared beam from a transmitter to a receiver. The market for these sensors can be categorized into two main types: digital signal and analog signal. Digital signal sensors provide binary output, indicating the presence or absence of an object, ideal for simple applications. In contrast, analog signal sensors offer continuous output proportional to the distance or size of detected objects, enabling more precise measurement and control in complex automation tasks.</p></p>
