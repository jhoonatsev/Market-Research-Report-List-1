<p><p>The Large Aperture Scintillometer market features various lens types that enhance optical performance for atmospheric measurements. Plano-convex lenses are designed with one flat and one curved surface, providing a focused beam with minimal optical distortion, ideal for long-distance light collection. Fresnel lenses, on the other hand, are thinner and lighter, using concentric rings to capture and direct light efficiently. Both lens types are crucial for improving the accuracy of scintillometer readings in environmental monitoring applications.</p></p>
